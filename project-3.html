Knapsack Problem Solutions — Queens College: Genetic Algorithms

Knapsack Problem Solutions — Queens College: Genetic Algorithms

Designed three Java programs solving the knapsack problem, where one must determine the best selection of items from a collection with maximum value under a weight limit. Created the first approach in brute force generating all possible solutions to find the best, second in random search generating 100 generations of 1000 random solutions for the final one. Developed the third approach in genetic algorithms; over each generation of 1000 solutions, top solutions breed to make the next generation, the final solution being the best in the 100th.
