What I Bring

I am a builder. I have a massive repertoire of coding knowledge that can allow me to build massive projects through a variety of coding languages and technologies, from LISP to C++ to HTML and so on. But I also can build beyond the machine, whether it be building connections, or building an enriching environment for all.

I am a team-player. The key to assembling complex projects nowadays is extensive coordination between team members, and to do so requires respect for ones peers, delineation, and keeping a keen eyes for flaws in oneself or your peers to aid them. I have these in spades and much more. My part-time background in education has familiarized me with the notions of simplifying complex topics to those unfamiliar with them. I can provide key leadership whether due to emergency or structured planning. I can detect patterns quickly and organize on a tight schedule. Being a team player is about bringing the best of yourself to the table while taking into account whether you may fall.

I am a learner. One of the biggest legal sins in work is arrogance. I don't know everything and don't claim to. Preserving the mindset of a learner means I can adapt on the fly, ask questions when needed, and practice new skills and topics by myself while avoiding burdening my peers. Education does not end with a diploma.
